Honors senior thesis work: A New Probabilistic Model to Evaluate the Age and Erosion Rate of Young Sedimentary Rock on Mars

For my honors senior thesis project at UChicago (completed May 2021), I wrote a model based on the Poisson equation to estimate the formation age and erosion rate of the Medusae Fossae Formation (MFF) on Mars with Edwin Kite as my advisor. A large sedimentary unit located at the equatorial region, the MFF has an uncertain formation process and age. Due to a low dielectric constant and the induration necessary for this sedimentary unit to form, the MFF has interesting implications for both potential subsurface ice and when there was recent liquid water in the martian climate. Past work

During my undergraduate career, I had the opportunity to explore a variety of research projects. This included my NASA Student Airborne Research Program (SARP) project "Salton Sea Chlorophyll: A Case Study of How Biology Responds to an Increasingly Hypersaline Environment" with Raphael Kudela at UCSC, a technical review paper about perchlorates on Mars with Pascal Lee at the SETI Institute, environmental quantum computing applications with Gavin Jones at IBM Research, and testing a new detector for the South Pole Telescope with Clarence Chang at Argonne National Laboratory. In undergrad I was excited (and still am!) about a lot of different Earth and space-related topics, so I tried a bunch of things before figuring out what was most fascinating for me.
